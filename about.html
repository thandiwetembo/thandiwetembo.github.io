Hello!


Im Thandiwe (pronounced tăn•DEE•wĕ)

I entered college thinking I would exit a software developer. Declaring a combined degree in Computer Science and Design, I wanted to nurture my analytical problem-solving skills and my creative passion, expanding both my right and left brain. Additionally, declaring a minor in Criminal Justice to learn more about people. Weirdly enough, this combination is the reason I exited university confident in becoming a UX Designer. Combining creative, analytical, and empathetic problem solving provided me with the foundation of what UX Designers do. It fostered my curiosity to analyze and enhance products and systems, my passion for collaborating with others on new challenges, and my excitement to understand and empathize with others and produce accessible and inclusive design that enhances the human experience and reflects the future of our society.

Design is my chosen tool to achieve meaningful change, connection & community.
I advocate for inclusive and accessible design that reflects embraces the diversity within society.

When I'm not fully immersed in the art & design world, you'll probably find me baking, cooking, weightlifting, or wandering around an art museums
