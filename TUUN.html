TUUN



TIMEFRAME | ~8 months

ROLE | UI/UX (Palette Conformation & Playlist), UXR, Testing

TEAM SIZE | 3 Developers + 4 Designers


01 | TLDR;

TUUN is a senior capstone project in which team designed and developed a web application in which users can create algorithmically curated playlists to explore their unique associations color and music.

02 | THE DESIGN CHALLENEG

“Create anything interactive”

Being given an open ended prompt allows for a lot of freedom but also little direction.

Collaborative brainstorming led us to start thinking about discovery and experiences with music and digital community. We concluded that we wanted to create a digital personalized music discovery experience for Spotify users and help users discover new music.

03 | OPPORTUNITY STATEMENT

Spotify’s Mission: to unlock the potential of human creativity—by giving a million creative artists the opportunity to live off their art and billions of fans the opportunity to enjoy and be inspired by it.

With over 80 million tracks on Spotify, the process of finding new music and curating personalized playlist can be over whelming to users. There is an opportunity to provide users with algorithmically curated playlist based on unexplored properties.

04 | DISCOVERY &

The discovery phase of developing a this product had 3 elements:
Research, Competitive Analysis, & Interviews.

The results of our research phase not only validated that building an experience around color-music-emotion would result in a personalized experience & playlists but also that there was a substantial number of possible users, a space for it in the music experience market.

Research

Spotify

The music platform of choice was Spotify because they have 31% of the global music streaming market in 2021 and 365 million monthly active users according to their Q2 2021 report. Additionally, it is a platform that the team knew well and access to testing participants and potential users would be abundant.

Music, Color, Emotion

We decided to design the experience around the color. We knew peoples taste and connection to music is not universal and often unique to the individual. Additionally, we know that music can evoke certain emotions and wanted to use emotion as the middle man between music and color because color can also evoke and relate to emotions. Additionally, all songs on Spotify are given a “valence” metric or a metic that describes “the musical positiveness conveyed by a track” and this would be a key in the backend of our experience.

Interviews

We lastly conducted interviews to gauge:

↳ How do people associate music to color (and is emotion a connecter between the two)?

↳ Are those associations unique or universal?

↳ Can these connections be used to produce a novel music discovery experience?

What we found was:

↳ Users commonly associated certain colors with certain moods/emotions (often falling in line with color psychology)

↳ Users often connected their favorite color and favorite artists to album cover art & emotion

↳ Connections between color and emotion were often related to Culture/Childhood/Experiences/Memories and Mood

↳ Users connected the same artist to different colors & same the color to artists in genres, suggesting a possibility of unique or personalized color-music associations

Competitive Analysis

A competitive analysis was done to understand how new experiences were being created using Spotify. We saw that there were tons of products that created custom playlists based on ones Spotify library and product that specifically used color and emotion to do that but none that used both music and color indicating a possible space in the market for our product.
